FAQs

Anticipated Q&A on the Offer:

The Offer:

1. What will the offer size be?

The offer size is anticipated to be Tzs 476bn.

2. What is the total number of shares on offer?

The total number of shares that will be issued is 560m shares. Post offer, this will equate to approximately 25% of the shares of Vodacom Tanzania.

3. What is the price of the shares?

Tzs 850 per share.

4. Who will be eligible to purchase the shares?

The offer is open to all Tanzanian individuals and Tanzanian corporate bodies or institutions. Those who are not residing in Tanzania at the time of the offer may be affected by the laws of the relevant jurisdictions in which they reside.

5. Are there a minimum number of shares that need to be purchased?

A minimum of 100 shares will need to be applied for by each applicant and multiples of 10 shares can be purchased thereafter

6. Are there a maximum number of shares which an applicant can apply for?

No, there is no maximum ceiling. In the event of an over-subscription, the offer shares will be allocated on a pro-rata basis.

7. When will the offer period commence and when will it close?

The offer will be open to the public from 08:00 9 March, 2017 and will close on the 19 April 2017 at 17:00.

8. Will customers receive a discount if they pay using M-PESA?

Unfortunately not.

Applying for Shares:

1. Where can application forms for the offer be obtained?

Application forms can be obtained at any National Bank of Commerce (“NBC”) branch or from the offices of any of the Dar Stock Exchange (“DSE”) approved brokers and collecting agents.

2. Where can payment for the shares be effected?

Payment for the shares will need to accompany your application form. This can be made to any of the approved brokers or collecting agents, or at any branch of NBC

3. Who are the collecting agents or brokers where I can apply and pay for the shares?

Authorised Collecting agents or brokers are licensed dealing members authorized by Vodacom Tanzania to procure subscriptions for offer shares. The list is available in the prospectus copies, as well as our website under the investor tab.

You can also pay for shares via M-Pesa upon registration on the DSE platform.

4. Will I be able to use the Vodacom network to purchase the shares, i.e. can I take my application and payment to a Vodacom store, or service desk?

Although designated staff at the Vodacom network of stores and kiosks can point you in the direction of where an application can be lodged and payment can be made, these points of contact will not be able to accept applications or payment. Only the approved brokers, collecting agents and NBC bank will be able to take an application and accompanying payment

5. Can applicants submit more than one application?

Yes.

6. Can I effect payment using M-Pesa and how will that work?

Yes. If you are a first time buyer, you will first need to register on DSE. You can do this by dialing into the DSE platform for registration.

To Register

· Dial *150*36#

Enter Name e.g. John Doe

· Press enter

Enter address e.g. P.O.Box 1234 DSM

· Press enter

· Select ID type: you can enter citizen ID, Voters ID, Passport, Driver’s license, or Office ID

· Press enter

Enter your ID number

You will receive a PIN and confirmation SMS. Upon successful registration, you can purchase shares via DSE menu.

To purchase shares:

  • Dial *150*36#.
  • You will see the menu:

1. Secondary market; companies already listed for trading on DSE

2. Primary markets; companies making Initial Public Offer

  • Select 2 for IPO purchase.
  • You will see options and prices for purchase
  • Select Vodacom IPO to purchase

Enter quantity e.g. 100

  • You will see a confirmation display

E.g.

Confirmation

Company: VTL

Shares: 100

Price: 1234

  • Select 1 to confirm.

You will receive an SMS with a reference number. Please keep this number safe and you will use this number to make payments for your purchase via M-Pesa.

To make payments on M-Pesa

· Dial *150*00#

· Select Lipa Kwa or Pay by MPesa

· Select Enter Business Number

· Enter Business number 236622 (this is the DSE collection account)

· Enter reference number received in the SMS

· You will receive confirmation that your transaction has been sent for processing with DSE.

Upon closing of the IPO, customers who have successfully made a purchase can collect their share certificates via channels

7. Can a single applicant submit multiple applications via the DSE platform?

Yes, an applicant can submit as many applications as he would like. Every application will have a different reference number.

8. Can I pay for multiple applications with a single transfer/ cheque?

You will be able to do this. However, you will need to clearly stipulate the particulars and account details of each of the applicants to the offer. This will ensure that allotments and refunds are effected to the appropriate parties

9. Can a single applicant submit multiple payments against a single reference number obtained from DSE mobile platform over multiple days?

For payments via M-Pesa using a reference number from the DSE platform, every reference is tied to a single specific amount, so you may only pay for the shares that you have applied for at that time. Normal M-PESA transaction limits will apply on all transactions.

8. Does a DSE reference number expire after some duration?

The reference number will be valid during the whole period of the IPO. It will only expire once the IPO closes.

9. Can an applicant make payment over the offer period?

Yes. However, if payment is being made via M-Pesa, then the entire payment sum must be paid in one payment, it cannot be staggered. For example, if you are to pay Tzs 850,000 for the purchase of 100 shares, then you must pay Tzs 850,000 in one payment. You will not be able to break this payment down in to smaller multiple sums.

10. Once the application is submitted, how much time does the applicant have to pay for the shares for which they applied?

After filling the application form the applicant must endeavor to immediately pay in cash or submit proof of payment, otherwise the application will not be considered valid.

Post Offer:

1. When will the allotment and offer results be announced?

The results of the offer will be announced once the Board of Vodacom Tanzania has approved the allotment of the shares.

2. How will shares be allocated to applicants?

Shares will be allocated at the discretion of the Board of Vodacom Tanzania subject to approval by CMSA.

3. When can customer expect refunds for shares which they have not been allocated?

After we have completed allocation and received approval from CMSA, the lead receiving bank will start the refund process to the applicant’s bank account or mobile numbers.

For queries about the refund for an application made through a broker, please contact your broker. For payments made via M-Pesa please call our helpline.

4. How will Vodacom make refunds – which channels?

The refund will be done by the Lead receiving bank through the applicant’s bank account or phone number (mobile money payments).

5. When will the shares be listed?

The listing of the shares on the stock exchange will be done on 16 May 2017. Trading in the stock will be effected on the same day.

6. Is there a mandatory holding period or can I commence trading as soon as the listing is affected?

There is no mandatory holding period on the stock. Trading in the stock can begin immediately after the listing on 9 May 2017

7. When will my CSD account be credited?

Your CSD account will be credited just prior to listing, i.e. 15 May 2017

8. How will the post listing trading of the stock work?

Trading in the stock will be subject to the normal rules of the DSE post the listing.

Vodacom as a listed company:

1. Has Vodacom fulfilled all regulatory requirements?

Yes we have. Vodacom Tanzania is a duly incorporated public company and has adhered to all the regulatory requirements to be established as such
