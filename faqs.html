1. What will the offer size be?

The offer size is anticipated to be Tzs 476bn.

2. What is the total number of shares on offer?

The total number of shares that will be issued is 560m shares. Post offer, this will equate to approximately 25% of the shares of Vodacom Tanzania.

3. What is the price of the shares?

Tzs 850 per share.

4. Who will be eligible to purchase the shares?

The offer is open to all Tanzanian individuals and Tanzanian corporate bodies or institutions. Those who are not residing in Tanzania at the time of the offer may be affected by the laws of the relevant jurisdictions in which they reside.

5. Are there a minimum number of shares that need to be purchased?

A minimum of 100 shares will need to be applied for by each applicant and multiples of 10 shares can be purchased thereafter

6. Are there a maximum number of shares which an applicant can apply for?

No, there is no maximum ceiling. In the event of an over-subscription, the offer shares will be allocated on a pro-rata basis.

7. When will the offer period commence and when will it close?

The offer will be open to the public from 08:00 9 March, 2017 and will close on the 19 April 2017 at 17:00

8. Will customers receive a discount if they pay using M-PESA?

Unfortunately not.


1. Where can application forms for the offer be obtained?

Application forms can be obtained in the prospectus document, which will be available at any National Bank of Commerce (“NBC”) branch or from the offices of any of the Dar Stock Exchange (“DSE”) approved brokers and collecting agents, and Vodacom Tanzania retail shops.

2. Where can payment for the shares be effected?

Payment for the shares will need to accompany your application form. This can be made to any of the approved brokers or collecting agents, or at any branch of NBC

3. Who are the collecting agents or brokers where I can apply and pay for the shares?

Authorised Collecting agents or brokers are licensed dealing members authorized by Vodacom Tanzania to procure subscriptions for offer shares

You can also pay for shares via M-Pesa upon registration on the DSE platform.

4. Will I be able to use the Vodacom network to purchase the shares, i.e. can I take my application and payment to a Vodacom store, or service desk ?

Although designated staff at the Vodacom network of stores and kiosks can point you in the direction of where an application can be lodged and payment can be made, these points of contact will not be able to accept an applications or payment. Only the approved brokers, collecting agents and NBC bank will be able to take an application and accompanying payment

5. Can applicants submit more than one application?

Yes

6. Can I effect payment using M-Pesa and how will that work?

Yes.

If you are a first time buyer, you will first need to register on DSE. Youca n do this by dialing into the DSE platform for registration.

To Register:

  • Dial *150*36#
  • Enter Name e.g. John Doe
  • Press enter
  • Enter address e.g. P.O.Box 1234 DSM
  • Press enter
  • Select ID type: you can enter citizen ID, Voters ID, Passport, Driver's license, or Office ID
  • Press enter
  • Enter your ID number
  • You will receive a PIN and confirmation SMS. Upon successful registration, you can purchase shares via DSE menu.

To purchase shares:

  • Dial *150*36#
  • Select 2 for IPO purchase
  • You will see options and prices for purchase
  • Select Vodacom IPO to purchase
  • Enter quantity e.g. 100
  • You will see a confirmation display
  • Select 1 to confirm

You will receive an SMS with a reference number. Please keep this number safe and you will use this number to make payments for your purchase via M-Pesa

1. When will the allotment and offer results be announced?

All allotments of shares will be completed on 28 April 2017. The results of the offer will be announced on 28 April 2017

2. How will shares be allocated to applicants?

Shares will be allocated at the discretion of Vodacom Tanzania, in collaboration with the lead Advisor in and Lead Receiving Bank (NBC).

3. When can customer expect refunds for shares which they have not been allocated?

After we have completed allocation and being approved by CMSA the lead receiving bank will start refund to the Applicants bank account or Mobile numbers

4. How will Vodacom make refunds – which channels?

The refund will be done by the Lead receiving bank through the applicant’s bank account or phone number (mobile money payments).

5. When will the shares be listed?

The listing of the shares on the stock exchange will be done on 16 May 2017. Trading in the stock will be effected on the same day.

6. Is there a mandatory holding period or can I commence trading as soon as the listing is affected?

There is no mandatory holding period on the stock. Trading in the stock can begin immediately after the listing on 9 May 2017

7. When will my CSD account be credited?

Your CSD account will be credited just prior to listing, i.e. 15 May 2017

8. How will the post listing trading of the stock work?

Trading in the stock will be subject to the normal rules of the DSE post the listing


1. Has Vodacom fulfilled all regulatory requirements?

Yes we have. Vodacom Tanzania is a duly incorporated public company and has adhered to all the regulatory requirements to be established as such

2. What is the anticipated yield on the stock?

Ultimately, the directors of the company will determine if any dividend will be paid on the stock. They will take this decision based on a number of factors, including company performance, future growth plans for the company etc. The anticipated yield will thus be affected by this, coupled with the share price performance
